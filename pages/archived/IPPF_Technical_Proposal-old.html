4. Understanding of the Assignment

IPPF has built, over successive phases, a Federation-wide analytics environment that already functions as a credible single source of truth: a Snowflake enterprise data warehouse fed through Azure Data Factory, drawing on eight institutional systems from DHIS2 service-delivery data to NetSuite financials, and surfaced through Power BI. That environment was designed, sensibly, for the Secretariat. The task now is different in kind, not merely in degree: to open that same data to roughly 120 Member Associations and a wider network of Collaborative Partners, across some 150 countries and four working languages, in a form they will actually use. We understand this assignment as the bridge between an analytically mature Secretariat platform and the operational reality of a diverse, decentralised Federation.

That framing carries three implications that shape everything we propose:

01

First, this is an extension, not a rebuild.

The existing architecture is sound and represents significant prior investment. Our role is to build the Affiliate-facing layer on top of it — new views, new access structures, new language support — while strengthening integration and data quality where that serves the Affiliate use cases. The Secretariat dashboards continue to operate alongside what we build.

02

Second, the hard problems are about access and trust, not visualisation.

Secretariat users authenticate through IPPF organisational accounts and single sign-on; Affiliate users sit outside that domain entirely, which makes external identity and access management a central design question rather than a deployment afterthought. Equally, a Federation-wide dataset raises real governance questions: who sees what, and how benchmarking parameters are configured.

03

Third, adoption is the real measure of success.

A dashboard that is launched but not used delivers nothing. Across 150 countries the Federation contains enormous variation in connectivity, data literacy, language, and analytical capacity. A single uniform rollout will reach the well-resourced Affiliates and lose the rest. The assignment therefore demands a tiered training and adoption model designed for that variation.

5. Approach & Methodology

Our approach runs across the three Parts defined in the Terms of Reference, with two threads woven through all of them: continuous stakeholder validation, so that no major decision is taken without the people it affects; and iterative refinement, so that designs, builds, and training materials improve in response to real feedback.

1

Part 1 - Needs Analysis and Functional Specification

We begin with an inception phase to align with the Secretariat teams (PLI, MADDT, IT) on objectives, confirm the workplan, agree the consultation approach, and conduct an initial review of the existing environment. This produces the Inception Report and Workplan.

The needs analysis itself uses a tiered consultation model designed for a Federation too large to engage uniformly. We propose a structured digital survey issued to all Affiliates in the four working languages, capturing current data use, decision-making needs, reporting priorities, technical capacity, and connectivity. From that stratification we select a representative sample across maturity levels, regions, and languages for deeper engagement. Indicatively, this comprises a survey to all ~120 Member Associations, approximately 30-45 deep-dive interviews across the maturity tiers, and consultations with around 10-15 Secretariat stakeholders.

Finally we consolidate all validated outputs into the Functional Specification Document: priority use cases, data requirements and indicators, dashboard structure and functionality, user-experience principles, and the governance and access framework.

2

Part 2 - Development, Implementation and Iteration

Part 2 opens with implementation planning: we validate the technical approach against the existing architecture, confirm dependencies, risks, and integration requirements.

We then strengthen the data environment. We review and validate the existing Snowflake and Azure Data Factory architecture, strengthen and expand integration across the source systems, validate indicator definitions, and address data-quality issues. Dashboard development then proceeds within the existing Power BI environment: building the Affiliate-facing views, visualisations, and navigation, implementing role-based access and row-level security, and building in multilingual support. We anticipate an approach based on Microsoft Entra External ID with a licensing model appropriate to Federation scale.

3

Part 3 - Rollout, Training and Adoption

Part 3 is where the investment is realised or lost, and we treat it with corresponding weight. Our structured methodology is detailed in Section 6.

Cross-cutting: governance, risk, and ways of working

Throughout, we work in close collaboration with the Secretariat teams, participate in regular check-ins, and present prototypes. We maintain a live risk register from inception covering data quality variances, right-to-left alignment limits in Power BI, licensing complexities, and stakeholder collaboration.

6. Training, Rollout and Adoption

We have built our approach around the diversity of the Federation rather than around an average Affiliate that does not exist. The needs-analysis survey in Part 1 stratifies Affiliates by data maturity, connectivity, and capacity. That same stratification drives the rollout, through three tiers.

Tier 1

Comprises the higher-maturity Affiliates with existing data capacity and reliable connectivity. These receive advanced, direct training early during the pilot stage and become regional peer anchors.

Tier 2

Comprises the broad middle of the Federation. Onboarding is delivered through peer support combined with self-paced modules on the IPPF Academy, reinforced by scheduled virtual webinars.

Tier 3

Comprises lower-capacity and lower-connectivity Affiliates. They receive simplified, role-focused offline-friendly guides, low-bandwidth materials, and direct targeted adoption support.

For each defined user role we develop targeted materials: concise role-based user guides, short video walkthroughs, and quick-reference cards. All materials are produced in English, French, Spanish, and Arabic, and hosted on the IPPF Academy (Moodle) and the MA Forum.

During the initial adoption period (indicatively eight weeks), we provide structured troubleshooting, check-ins, and gather ongoing feedback to iterate upon dashboard styling, usability, and configuration settings.
